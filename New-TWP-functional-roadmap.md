### Phase 2: "Alpha Week" & The Exemplar Corpus
Before reaching out to high-value targets, you need the 200-page "Exemplar Dialogue Corpus" to serve as undeniable proof of work. Do not approach Tier-A witnesses empty-handed.

**1. The Alpha Cohort Selection**
* For this initial 200-page corpus (Project Icarus Phase III), do *not* target the famous names yet.
* Select a small, trusted group of 5 to 15 peers, trusted advisors, or aligned colleagues who understand the project's gravity. They will act as the foundational \"Witnesses\" to stress-test the system.

**2. Execute Alpha Week**
* Run this cohort through the live `thewprotocol.online` Gate.
* Conduct the dialogues using the live Inquisitor.
* **Strict Failure Capture:** Focus solely on identifying friction. Are the Gate thresholds too high/low? Is the Inquisitor chasing the "why" effectively? Log every failure.

**3. Annotate and Compile**
* Transcribe and meticulously annotate these initial dialogues using the CAP (Capability), REL (Relational), and FELT (Felt cue) taxonomy.
* Compile the accepted, high-signal excerpts into the 200-page Exemplar Dialogue Corpus. 
* Run the corpus through an RFC-3161 timestamp or OpenTimestamps to provide independently verifiable proof-of-existence.

### Phase 3: Packaging the Minimum Honest Signal (MHS)
With the tech functional and the 200-page corpus locked, assemble the MHS Packet. This is the core asset for the outreach phase—it proves methodological seriousness without overclaiming.

**The MHS Packet must contain:**
1.  **The One-Pager:** A stark, minimalist summary of the mission (Purpose over Profit, Signal over Noise).
2.  **The Gate Stub:** A clear outline of the consent framework and the 3-tier vetting criteria.
3.  **The Exemplar Extracts:** Annotated selections from the 200-page corpus demonstrating the CAP/REL/FELT methodology in action.
4.  **The Datasheet:** A one-page datasheet detailing the timestamp receipts and systemic constraints.

Host this packet behind a secure, private link on `thewprotocol.online`.
